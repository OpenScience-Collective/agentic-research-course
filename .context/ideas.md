# Agentic Research Course -- Design Ideas

## Core Pedagogical Vision
**Goal:** Teach researchers that AI coding agents are transformative, and that structured workflows (git, QA/QC, version control) are the foundation that makes AI tools safe and productive.

**Key Principles:**
- AI tools are extremely powerful; that power demands quality control
- Git and version control are the safety net that lets you move fast with AI
- Every session should produce something tangible (a commit, a figure, a draft)
- The practicum (HBN analysis) makes abstract concepts concrete

## Landscape Framing (Week 1)
The first session should set the stage: what is available, what is most important, and why.
- AI coding agents (Claude Code, Copilot, Cursor) have changed the game
- The more powerful the tool, the more you need guardrails
- Git is not just version control; it is your undo button, your audit trail, your collaboration layer
- Show the 8x productivity chart from the blog post (1,052 to 8,563 contributions)

## Content Threading
The HBN practicum should thread through every session:
- Week 2: Set up project, create CLAUDE.md for HBN analysis
- Week 3: Plan the analysis as an epic with phases
- Week 4: Add CI to the analysis pipeline
- Week 5: Literature review on shot-change ERPs
- Week 6: Grant section on the analysis approach
- Week 7: Methods section for the analysis
- Week 8: ERP figures
- Week 9: BIDS conversion of HBN data
- Week 10: Custom plugin for the analysis workflow

## Deliverable Ideas
- Step-by-step guidelines document for each session
- Blog posts summarizing key sessions
- Presentation slides/outlines for Discord Stage
- Video recordings on YouTube

## Target Audience Considerations
- Researchers who may never have used a terminal
- Mix of neuroscience, psychology, engineering backgrounds
- Some will be experienced coders wanting to add AI to their workflow
- Progressive complexity: weeks 1-2 assume nothing, weeks 9-10 assume competence
