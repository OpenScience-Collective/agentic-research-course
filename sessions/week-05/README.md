# Week 5: Literature Search and Review

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will:
- Search academic literature across multiple databases using opencite
- Explore citation graphs and find canonical papers
- Download PDFs and convert them to markdown for analysis
- Write cohesive literature reviews with AI assistance
- Understand citation weaving and thematic organization

## Outline

### Part 1: Academic Search with opencite (15 min)
- Installing and configuring opencite
- Searching Semantic Scholar, OpenAlex, PubMed
- Finding canonical/foundational papers
- Exploring citation graphs (who cites whom)

### Part 2: PDF Retrieval and Conversion (10 min)
- Downloading PDFs by DOI
- Batch fetching with `batch-fetch --convert`
- Markdown conversion for AI-readable analysis
- BibTeX export for citation management

### Part 3: Writing Literature Reviews (15 min)
- The difference between annotated bibliography and synthesis
- Thematic vs chronological organization
- Citation weaving patterns (integral, non-integral, synthesis, contrast)
- Identifying gaps: "Despite these advances..."
- Common pitfalls: string-of-pearls, overclaiming, recency bias

### Part 4: Live Demo (5 min)
- Search for papers in our practicum's research area
- Synthesize findings into a mini literature review

### Q&A (15 min)

## Key Concepts

- **Citation graph:** The network of papers citing and cited by a given paper
- **Canonical papers:** The most-cited, foundational work on a topic
- **Thematic organization:** Grouping papers by theme, not by paper
- **Citation weaving:** Integrating references into prose naturally
- **Gap statement:** Articulating what the literature hasn't addressed
