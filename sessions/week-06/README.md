# Week 6: Grant Proposal Writing

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will:
- Understand NIH and NSF funding mechanisms and their requirements
- Draft a Specific Aims page with AI assistance
- Write Research Strategy sections (Significance, Innovation, Approach)
- Self-review proposals using simulated study section scoring
- Check figures for NIH/NSF compliance

## Outline

### Part 1: Funding Mechanisms (10 min)
- NIH: R01, R21, K99, K08, F31, F32
- NSF: CAREER, standard grants
- Page limits, formatting requirements, submission timelines
- What reviewers actually look for

### Part 2: Specific Aims (15 min)
- The anatomy of a Specific Aims page
- Opening paragraph: hook, gap, objective
- Aims: hypothesis-driven, measurable, independent
- Impact statement
- Live drafting with `/grant-write`

### Part 3: Research Strategy (10 min)
- Significance: why does this matter?
- Innovation: what's new about your approach?
- Approach: how will you do it?
- Preliminary data and feasibility
- Timeline and milestones

### Part 4: Self-Review and Figure QA (10 min)
- Using `/grant-review` to simulate a study section
- NIH scoring: 1-9 scale across 5 criteria
- Figure quality: resolution, fonts, colorblind safety
- Budget justification basics

### Q&A (15 min)

## Key Concepts

- **Specific Aims:** The 1-page roadmap of your proposal (most important page)
- **Study section:** The peer review panel that scores your proposal
- **Significance:** The importance of the problem you're addressing
- **Innovation:** What's new about your approach vs the state of the art
- **Approach:** Your detailed research plan with methods, timeline, and contingencies
